About IXLAB ✨


Our research focuses on Human-Computer Interaction and Human-centered AI. IXLAB aims to design and optimize intelligent interactive systems that deeply understand and adapt to human users. Our approach is based on three core processes:

(2) Modeling â€” analyzing and inferring latent behavioral and contextual patterns through data-driven models and model personalization, and
(3) Adapting â€” enabling systems to respond intelligently and supportively in real-time to users.

We employ methods including ML/DL, data-driven user modeling, multimodal signal processing, and sensor fusion to build systems that are not only technically robust but also meaningfully responsive to human needs.   • The 2026 Winter Undergraduate Research Internship will open after midterm exam in the fall semester.

We are looking for passionate students who enjoy identifying and solving real-world problems from the perspective of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). Our goal is to quantitatively measure and evaluate the invisible interactions between humans and computers, ultimately optimizing these interactions. We build data-driven models that predict user behavior and performance, and based on these models, we develop systems that enhance interaction efficiency.

IXLAB prioritizes building and advancing each student's individual research area in the field of human-computer interaction through a structured onboarding and mentoring process. We are looking for passionate students who enjoy identifying issues in phenomena that others might overlook, and who are eager to engage in discussion to solve them.

While engaging in research can be an incredibly rewarding experience, deciding whether to pursue a master's or Ph.D. program is not an easy choice. A good fit between the student and advisor is just as important as the research topic itself for a fulfilling research journey. We strongly recommend joining our undergraduate intern program to gain firsthand experience working with the IX Lab advisor. If you are interested in joining IXLAB, please feel free to contact eunjipark(at)cau.ac.kr.
